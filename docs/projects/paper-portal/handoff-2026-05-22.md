## Creative Goal

The video starts as a real hand-drawn sketch hook. The user draws a simple stick
figure and door on paper. The video speeds through the drawing, snaps/flashes
into a cleaned pencil animation, then the stick figure walks through portals
into visually different worlds.

The current story shape:

1. Real drawing hook.
2. Pencil sketch animation: stick figure walks to the door.
3. Door/portal transition.
4. Pixel world: stick figure walks into a glowing pixel portal.
5. More worlds can follow through the same video/edit pattern.

The user wants the worlds to feel markedly different, but not become a messy
style demo. Five worlds is probably enough.

Recommended world count:

1. Pencil sketch world.
2. Pixel world.
3. Soft 3D storybook / toy-like world.
4. Anime or hand-painted Japanese fantasy world.
5. Stop-motion clay, paper cutout, watercolor, neon arcade, or plush diorama.

## What Worked

- The live drawing hook works better when kept around 12 seconds with source
  audio, a gradual speed ramp, and no overly complicated early zoom.
- The hand/tap moment works as the transition point into the cleaned seed image.
- The cleaned first-half handoff uses a blur/white flash and short anchor to
  avoid a weird camera drift.
- Seedance regular produced the best pencil walk-to-handle motion.
- The best AI-only pencil sequence came from stitching:
  - `seedance-regular-walk-to-handle-v02.mp4`
  - `seedance-regular-door-transition-variant3-v02-surprise.mp4`
- Cutting/cleaning the Seedance sequence helped:
  - Removed dead still-image hold.
  - Added soft blend between walk and transition.
  - Trimmed ending when the figure disappeared.
- Flow/Veo with start and end images worked better than trying to use video as
  a motion reference.
- Simpler prompts worked better than complex prompts:
  - No surprise beat, or a very restrained "happy and curious" beat.
  - Hero walks steadily left to right.
  - Mascots mostly stay still.
  - Portal glow stays contained.
- The liked pixel-world candidate is in:
  `finalized-working-set/03-pixel-world-walks-into-portal-current-candidate.mp4`.

## What Failed Or Was Risky

- Omni/Flow video-to-video style transfer often ignored the video, hallucinated
  unrelated content, or triggered policy/unusual activity errors.
- Flow prompt errors appeared around some combinations of:
  - long negative prompt blocks
  - brand/style names
  - possibly the uploaded video itself
  - words like "portal" or "disappear" may have contributed in some cases, but
    this was not proven
- "Minecraft" and exact brand/studio names should be avoided in prompts.
  Describe the style instead.
- Start/end frames that are not compositionally aligned cause jumps.
- The "surprise/whoa" beat caused overacting and too much model improvisation.
  The cleaner direction is: hero happily walks, looks around, gives one small
  wave, enters portal.
- Broad ImageGen edits of the end frame moved mascots, removed characters, or
  changed the whole image. Use masks for precise edits.
- Extension prompts in Flow did not work well for moving into a new world.
  Better path: use edit mode on the existing video or generate a separate short
  clip with start/end references.

## Current Prompt Pattern For Pixel Start/End

Use this when generating the pixel-world walk-through from start and end frames:

```text
Use the first image as the start frame and the second image as the end frame.

Create one simple locked-off side-view pixel-art animation.

The small stick-figure hero walks steadily from left to right toward the glowing portal. He is happy and curious, gently looking around as he walks. As he passes the friendly characters, he gives one small casual wave, then keeps walking forward into the portal.

The hero keeps the same simple shape: round head, simple smile, skinny stick body, black outline, readable old-cartoon walking steps. He must not walk backward, jump, float, glow, or turn into a silhouette.

The three friendly pixel characters near the portal are important fixed characters. Keep all three visible for the entire clip: the green character, the orange hat character, and the blue character. They stay in their same positions near the portal and remain inside the frame. They do not disappear, merge into the portal glow, walk away, or move out of frame.

The friendly characters only make tiny idle motions, like a small wave or slight bounce. They should not walk, jump, chase the hero, or block the path.

The hero simply walks into the glowing portal and is gone by the final frame. The final frame should match the uploaded end image: hero gone, portal still glowing, all three friendly characters still visible near the portal.

Keep the portal glow soft and contained inside the doorway. Do not create a large explosion, smoke cloud, burst, full-screen flash, or glowing silhouette.

Keep the camera completely static. Keep the same side-view composition, pixel-art world, portal position, ground path, and bright colorful style.

No camera movement, no zoom, no crop change, no disappearing mascots, no missing blue character, no explosion, no big flash, no silhouette transformation, no extra characters, no text, no logos.
```

## Omni / Flow Edit Core Lock Prompt

Use this when editing an existing video into a new style. This is better than
Flow "extend" for now.

```text
Edit the uploaded video. Use the video as the exact motion, timing, framing, and composition reference.

Keep the same locked side-view camera, same portal position, same ground path, same hero path, same three side characters, and same ending. Do not reframe, zoom, crop, pan, or change the camera.

The stick-figure hero keeps the same action: he walks left to right, gives a small friendly wave, enters the glowing portal, and is gone by the end.

The three friendly characters near the portal must stay visible in their same positions for the entire video. They do not follow the hero, vanish, merge into the portal, or move out of frame. They only do tiny idle motions and a small wave.

Keep the portal glow soft and contained. No explosion, no giant flash, no full-screen light, no silhouette transformation.
```

Add one style block below the core lock prompt.

## Stitched Render Template

Use this pattern when stitching two video files with mismatched fps/resolution:

```bash
ffmpeg -y -i INPUT_A.mp4 -i INPUT_B.mp4 \
  -filter_complex "[0:v]scale=1920:1080:force_original_aspect_ratio=decrease,pad=1920:1080:(ow-iw)/2:(oh-ih)/2,setsar=1,fps=30,format=yuv420p[v0];[1:v]scale=1920:1080:force_original_aspect_ratio=decrease,pad=1920:1080:(ow-iw)/2:(oh-ih)/2,setsar=1,fps=30,format=yuv420p[v1];[0:a]aformat=sample_fmts=fltp:sample_rates=48000:channel_layouts=stereo[a0];[1:a]aformat=sample_fmts=fltp:sample_rates=48000:channel_layouts=stereo[a1];[v0][a0][v1][a1]concat=n=2:v=1:a=1[v][a]" \
  -map "[v]" -map "[a]" \
  -c:v libx264 -preset slow -crf 18 \
  -c:a aac -b:a 192k \
  -movflags +faststart OUTPUT.mp4
```
