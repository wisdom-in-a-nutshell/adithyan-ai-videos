PAGE-TURN-TO-BLANK template (Kling O3, 5s, 1:1)
Used for: ending an age beat by turning the page to a clean blank cream page.

Substitute {SUBJECT_DESCRIPTION} for the previous portrait subject (e.g.
"a young boy", "a young man with curly hair", etc.).

---

Locked camera, full-frame view of a hand-drawn pencil and watercolor portrait of {SUBJECT_DESCRIPTION} on cream sketchbook paper.

PAGE-TURN motion — turning to the next blank page in a sketchbook:

[0.0 – 0.7s]: Hold on the portrait.

[0.7 – 1.5s]: The right edge of the page begins to LIFT and curl gently upward, as if a hand or breeze is turning the page. The portrait starts to peel away from the right side.

[1.5 – 3.0s]: The page TURNS — the entire portrait page lifts up and rotates over toward the left, sliding out of the frame. As the page lifts, the BLANK CREAM PAGE underneath is revealed.

[3.0 – 4.5s]: The previous page finishes turning out of frame. The blank cream sketchbook page settles flat into view, completely empty, ready to be drawn on. Same cream paper tone as before.

[4.5 – 5.0s]: Hold on the blank cream page.

Critical: this is a real PAGE-TURN motion — paper physically lifting, curling, rotating away to reveal a blank page underneath. Like turning the next page in a real sketchbook. NOT a fade, NOT a dissolve. Real paper-page-turning physics.

Constraints: locked camera, no zoom, no pan. NO visible hand or fingers — the page turns on its own. Single continuous take. The clip MUST end exactly at the provided end frame (a clean blank cream page) for seamless stitching.
