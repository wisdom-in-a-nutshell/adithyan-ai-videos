# Paper Portal Storyboard

## Core idea

A rough hand-drawn scene on paper becomes an animated world.

Adi films himself drawing a simple stick figure and a door from above. Once the sketch is complete, the live paper shot freezes or settles. A clean vector version quietly aligns over the drawing, then the stick figure starts moving. It walks toward the door. As it crosses through, the rough monochrome sketch turns into a colorful, polished world.

## Feeling to test

- handmade → alive
- rough idea → moving prototype
- simple pencil sketch → colorful payoff
- playful, magical, builder-y, not overproduced

## First prototype scope

Keep this intentionally small:

1. Use a simple paper sketch: stick figure, floor line, door.
2. Fade from live/frozen sketch into a clean SVG layer.
3. Move the figure as a basic rigged SVG puppet.
4. Reveal color through/after the door.
5. Stop as soon as the visual feeling is proven or disproven.

## Open questions

- Does the real-paper-to-SVG transition feel magical or cheap?
- Should the figure move as a rigged SVG puppet, frame sprites, or a hybrid?
- How much hand-drawn wobble/jitter makes it feel alive without becoming messy?
- Should AI-generated SVGs enter the pipeline now, or only after the manual prototype works?
